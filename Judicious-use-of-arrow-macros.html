E.12 Judicious use of “arrow” (->, ->>, etc...) macros

We sometimes over-use the -> and ->> macros. These typically only make sense to chain multiple calls, not single (or often double) nested call. One exception here is to aid indentation (avoid going over 80 characters) when regular nesting would require contortionist indentation. For example in the following excerpt from our tests. This 

    (is (equalp (->> (stmt-starting-with-text *collatz* "int collatz")
                     (function-body *collatz*))
                #|...|#))

is nicer than this. 

    (is (equalp (function-body
                 *collatz*
                 (stmt-starting-with-text *collatz* "int collatz"))
                #|...|#))
